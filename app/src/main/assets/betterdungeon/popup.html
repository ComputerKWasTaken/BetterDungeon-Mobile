BetterDungeon v2.0.0 Android
What's New Android version now available, bug fixes & more
Latest
Ultrascripts: My new two-way communication system between AI Dungeon scripts and BetterDungeon, giving scripts SUPERPOWERS, like calling AI models or rendering UI widgets. The natural replacement to BetterScripts, Ultrascripts no longer requires DOM manipulation, uses up story context, or requires the script creator to manually disable their systems when BetterScripts isn't available. It's smarter, more capable, and the future of scripting.
Android Port: Welcome to BetterDungeon Mobile! It's my custom WebView implementation that brings the full desktop experience to your Android device.
Firefox Port: BetterDungeon has been officially released on to the Firefox Browser Add-ons Store! No more reinstalling it as a temporary extension, it's here to stay.
Custom Dynamic: Your very own, customizable version of the Dynamic models is here! Choose from any available model, configure your pool, and have fun. Directly inspired by the great work of Zoocata's PRISM browser extension.
Text to Speech: Your story, narrated like an audio book. Select a voice, configure the settings, and enjoy listening to your story as you play. Will likely be seeing improvements in the future.
Revamped UI: A redesigned UI for all features and the popup menus, looking sleeker and less smelly. It's also got reduced clutter and is easier to navigate.
Revamped Markdown: Markdown has been revamped from the ground up to be more intelligent, easier to use, and have less refusals. High quality story formatting without any compromises, with 6 new instructions sets and a rewritten backend to maximize usage.
Revamped Character Presets: Rebuilt from the ground up and powered by my AI module from Ultrascripts, Character Presets takes away the burden of having to answer 47 scenario prefill questions, by leveraging the power of AI to intelligently answer the questions proposed.
Revamped Auto See: Using a reworked GQL backend, Auto See has been improved to be less fragile and disruptive, feeling much more natural and preventing blockages. Will likely be seeing improvements in the future.
Revamped Story Card Scanner: Using the magic of GQL, the Story Card Scanner is now instant, non-disruptive, and more intelligent. Less fragile and capable of handling even scenarios upwards of many hundreds of story cards. A massive plus for anybody designing scenarios.
Revamped Trigger Highlighting: Thanks to the revamped Story Card Scanner, trigger highlighting is now instant and enabled in all scenarios.
Revamped Story Card Analytics: Another improvement due to the revamped Story Card Scanner, the Story Card Analytics is instant and improved. Underrated feature that's now even better.
Ultrascripts Widget Module: Originally what BetterScripts was, Widget is now one piece of the many capabilities of Ultrascripts. Now featuring interactive widgets for elements like interactable buttons, better sizing, mobile support, and the ability to collapse widgets.
Ultrascripts WebFetch Module: Allows scripts powered by Ultrascripts to send GET requests, and basic search. Allowed domains operated by the users of the script. Will likely be seeing improvements in the future.
Ultrascripts Clock Module: Clock gives scripts the user's current local time, IANA time-zone conversion, ISO timestamps, offsets, and deterministic formatted dates, for real time grounding. Imagine an AI Dungeon scenario, synced to your real time in the world.
Ultrascripts SDK Module: The SDK module allows scripts to get toggle and configuration info about BetterDungeon, excluding secrets. Enables scripts to intelligently alter their behavior depending on what features are available.
Ultrascripts AI Module: The magnum opus of Ultrascripts, the AI module allows scripts to asynchronously query the Gemini API. No need to hijack the story or embed zero-width encoded messages, like AC or IS. The AI module also powers the revamped Character Presets feature. Highly recommend you all check this out, it is incredibly cool.
Ultrascripts Geolocation Module: If consented, allows AI Dungeon scripts to get the user's approximate location, to ground the scenario in the user's real life location. Must go through the standard Chrome/Firefox request popup.
Ultrascripts Weather Module: Allows scripts to get the real life weather data for locations, without the need for web scraping. Enables AI Dungeon scripts to be synced with real weather conditions.
Ultrascripts Network Module: Allows scripts to request online status and network quality information. Not sure what this might be used for but it's here if scripters want it.
Ultrascripts System Module: Enables scripts to get info such as device class, platform family, locale, screen size, touch support, browser family, and battery state, to enable scripts to alter its behavior depending on the system/device the user is on.

Command Mode

Send narrative commands to the AI

Adds a Command button to the input menu. Text is formatted as ## Your Command: for direct AI instructions.

Great for time skips, scene changes, and guiding story direction.

Standard## Your Command:
Subtle[## Your Command:]
OOC((OOC ... | User: Your Question?))

Use ↑↓ arrow keys while in Command mode to switch between Standard, Subtle, and OOC sub-modes.

Standard — direct story instructions. Subtle — indirect guidance via brackets. OOC — ask the AI a direct question.

Try Mode

Roll for action success or failure

RNG determines if actions succeed or fail. Formats as: You try to [action], you [result].

Outcomes: critically fail, fail, succeed, critically succeed.

Critical Chance 5%

Input History

Cycle through past inputs with arrow keys

Use the touch-friendly history controls beside the action input to cycle through recently sent inputs for the current adventure.

Remembers up to 50 of your recent actions and their respective input modes.

Widget

                  

                  
Live visual UI for script-driven displays

                

                
              

              

                
The Widget module lets adventure scripts render interface elements outside the story text, including stat bars, counters, panels, status readouts, and custom HTML widgets.

                
 Useful for RPG HUDs, relationship meters, timers, inventory panels, and scenario-specific dashboards.

              

            


            

              

                

                  

                    
                      
                    
                    
WebFetch

                  

                  
Consent-gated web requests and simple search

                

                
              

              

                
WebFetch gives scripts conservative web access for live context: safe GET requests, basic search, origin allow/deny decisions, rate limits, and blocked local/private targets.

                
 Useful for current facts, public reference pages, lightweight lookups, and web-aware scripted tools.

Clock

                  

                  
Reliable time, time zones, and formatting

                

                
              

              

                
Clock gives scripts the user's current local time, IANA time-zone conversion, ISO timestamps, offsets, and deterministic formatted dates.

                
 Useful for daily events, timed encounters, schedules, cooldowns, holidays, and world clocks.

              

            


            

              

                

                  

                    
                      
                    
                    
SDK

                  

                  
Version and configuration context for scripts

                

                
              

              

                
SDK gives scripts curated BetterDungeon metadata that complements heartbeat without duplicating Ultrascripts discovery, including version info and safe configuration context.

                
 Useful when a script needs to react to how the player has BetterDungeon configured while leaving heartbeat as the one module/ops truth.

World & Device Context

        

        

          

            

              

                

                  

                    
                      
                    
                    
Geolocation

                  

                  
Permission-based real-world location context

                

                
              

              

                
Geolocation lets scripts request the user's browser-approved location, returning coordinates, accuracy, and permission state only after the normal browser prompt allows it.

                
 Useful for local mysteries, travel scenes, real-world scavenger hooks, and location-aware setting details.

              

            


            

              

                

                  

                    
                      
                    
                    
Weather

                  

                  
Current conditions and forecast lookup

                

                
              

              

                
Weather gives scripts a focused weather path without general web scraping, using place names or coordinates to return current conditions and short forecasts.

                
 Useful for atmospheric scenes, survival mechanics, travel planning, seasonal events, and grounded local flavor.

              

            


            

              

                

                  

                    
                      
                    
                    
Network

                  

                  
Connection status and quality hints

                

                
              

              

                
Network reports online status and browser connection hints like effective type, round-trip time, downlink estimate, and data-saver preference when available.

                
 Useful for scripts that can choose lighter requests, avoid live lookups, or explain degraded behavior cleanly.

              

            


            

              

                

                  

                    
                      
                    
                    
System

                  

                  
Device, browser, display, and power context

                

                
              

              

                
System exposes broad environment details such as device class, platform family, locale, screen size, touch support, browser family, and battery state when supported.

                
 Useful for adapting script UI, reducing work on mobile or low-power devices, and tailoring experiences by device context.
